The Pilgrim's Progress from This World, to That Which Is to Come is a 1678 Christian allegory written by John Bunyan. It is commonly regarded as one of the most significant works of Protestant devotional literature and of wider early modern English literature .[1][2][3][4][5] It has been translated into more than 200 languages and has never been out of print.[6][7] It appeared in Ducth in 1681, in German in 1703 and in Swedish in 1727. The first North American edition was issued in 1681.
